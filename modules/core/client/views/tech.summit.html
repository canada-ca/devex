The Continuous Service Improvement (CSI) Lab is a space and a community that supports collaboration and the creation of solutions to high impact public challenges. In the Lab, teams typically composed of innovative public servants and leading private sector technologists, come together to use modern approaches and technology to address problems and ship solutions.

We move fast, connect with users, and use the latest tools to deliver results. Then, teams take their solutions, their learning and their community connections back to their home bases - whether that's in the public or private sector - to contribute to an expanding ecosystem of collaborators committed to using technology in the public interest.

In an effort to make it faster and easier for government and developers to work together, the BCDevExchange has created two new procurement processes:

Code With Us drives government to develop its digital products in the open and provides a simple way to acquire and pay developers for enhancements.

So far, over $700,000 worth of small opportunities ranging from $1,000 - $70,000 have been awarded to developers, many of whom have never worked for government.

Sprint With Us enables government to acquire an entire software development team within 30 calendar days for contracts of up to two years or $2M. Teams who apply get the opportunity to strut their stuff through a code challenge and, under a new Agile Software Development Agreement, are freed to bring their innovation to shape the product rather than being locked into delivering on a pre-determined set of requirements.

The new Developers Hub is the go-to place for government digital product teams to learn new skills, discover tools and resources, and connect with the developer community.

One of the key elements of the DevHub is the Design System—a collection of digital resources and tools, including reusable UI components and design patterns, aimed to help developers and designers build custom B.C government products.

The goal of the design system is to make it easier and faster to build B.C. government products while creating a consistent experience across services.

All DevHub components are collectively contributed to from the government community. They are built to meet government standards and are open for input and improvement. Building in the open ensures this resource is always relevant and up to date with the latest needs from the community.

The Integrated Data Division has partnered with LlamaZOO, through a Code With Us opportunity, to develop a prototype real-time data API service lightning strike and wildfire simulated data and Virtual Reality demonstration of the use of the APIs.

The project aims to help us understand the value of open data innovation in solving public sector challenges, increase awareness of new approaches to data visualization in the public sector and create new approaches to deliver real-time data through APIs for data visualization platforms.
